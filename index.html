NYC Shootings Sonification Map

This project explores how sound and spatial visualization can reveal patterns in public violence data that may be overlooked through charts or statistics alone.

Monthly shooting incidents across New York City are translated into sound using borough-specific synthesized instruments. Pitch reflects incident frequency (where higher pitch indicates more shooting incidents), while animated incident points and centroids trace the geographic distribution of shootings over time.

To emphasize periods of unusually high activity, a threshold-based percussive “shell hit” sound is introduced when monthly incidents exceed the citywide average (~50 incidents). The intensity of this sound increases as values rise further above the threshold, creating an additional auditory signal of elevated activity.

The interface is designed as both an analytical and reflective listening experience, encouraging users to navigate the data temporally, spatially, and sonically.

How to Use

Click a borough to focus the visualization and sonification. Use the Play button to hear the data unfold over time, or scrub the timeline to jump to specific months.

Created by Jurnee Brinson

Built with Mapbox GL JS, Tone.js, Turf.js, and GeoJSON using NYPD shooting incident data.

Data source: NYPD Shootings (2006-Present) Dataset
